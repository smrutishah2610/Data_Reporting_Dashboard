Table Overview

The table represents stock data for various companies, including essential details such as stock price, company name, number of stock sales, and whether the stock is in profit or loss. Here's a breakdown of the columns:

  1. Stock Name: The ticker symbol of the stock.
  2. Stock Price ($): The current price per share of the stock in dollars.
  3. Company Name: The full name of the company corresponding to the stock.
  4. No. of Stock Sales: The number of shares that have been sold.
  5. No. of Stock Not Sales: The number of shares that have not been sold (potentially referring to holdings).
  6. In Profit or Loss: Indicates whether the stock is currently in profit or loss compared to its purchase price.

How to Use the Sort Table

  1. Search: The `search` input allows users to search for specific stocks or companies within the table.
  2. Sortable Headers: Each column header is clickable (onclick="sortTable(n)"), allowing users to sort the table by that column's data.
  3. Data Representation: The table provides a clear and structured format for viewing essential stock data, making it easy to analyze and compare.
